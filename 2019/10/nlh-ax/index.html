Random NLH Strats with AXs

Oct 13, 2019

AKs - ATs

Balancing River Check Range

Suppose BTN open-raises and we call from SB with A♠T♠. The flop comes A♦6♥2♣. We check-call pot bet. The turn is 7♥ and action goes check check. The river is a Q♣. We can check at river:

  • Because the our AT is at the top our flop check-call range, we can then effectively balance our river check range. Therefore next time we may reach the showdown cheaply with mid/low pairs.
  • Induce bluff.
  • Induce Qx/pairs value bet.

A9s - A6s

Bet-Folding

Suppose we open from MP with A♦9♦ and CO calls. The flop is 9♠4♠8♦. We bet pot size and he calls. The turn is Q♥ and we bet 2/3 pot. CO raises.

  • At this time we should fold. Since it is ideal to to bet at turn to penalize all the drawing hands/missed draws and extract value from all the 9x/8x.
  • Your opponent with made hands will very likely to raise you, since the board is still wet.
  • On the other hand, a check-call route will expose your range and induce more bluff at river.

Check-Raising as Opponent’s Range Weakens

Suppose BTN open raises and we call at BB with A♠8♠. The flop is 10♥4♥2♥. Action goes check check at the flop, and the turn is Q♣. You check and he bets full-pot. You can check-raise here and check-fold if he calls.

The rational being that his range becomes narrower at the turn.

  • If he holds QQ/QT, he would tend to bet at flop to protect his hand.
  • If he holds suited ♥, he would tend to value bet at the flop.
  • If he holds single ♥, he would tend to bet protectively at the flop if it is large.

However, one disadvantage is that it is difficult to balance your range at river. As our range becomes highly polarized with only nuts or air, we may not bluff value-bet at the river with air unless your opponent folds very frequently at river. While we would 100% value bet at river with nuts.

3-Betting vs Calling A Check-Raise

Suppose we raise pre-flop with A♠6♠ and the CO calls. The flop goes 6♥6♦10♣. We c-bet and CO check-raises. If the CO is a TAG player, we should consider 3-betting more often to induce an all-in bluff. Since if you call his check-raise, he would usually quiet down after the turn.

A5s - A2s

Double-Barrel

Suppose we raise at BTN with A♠3♠ and SB calls. The flop is J♠8♦3♥. You c-bet and he check-calls. The turns is a non-J/8 ♠. SB checks. We should double-barrel to eliminate any 3x/8x/TT- with our nut flush draws and 14 outs.

On the other hand, if the turn is a J/8, you should check, as your turn value bet range has significantly narrows and the SB may call you more often. Moreover, your low pair has showdown value against missed straight draws.
